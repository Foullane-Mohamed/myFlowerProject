Monocotyledonous Weeds

Monocotyledonous weeds, such as Crabgrass and Johnson grass, have narrow leaves with parallel veins and a fibrous root system. They are fast-growing and can quickly compete with cultivated plants.

Dicotyledonous Weeds

Dicotyledonous weeds like Dandelion and Broadleaf plantain have broad leaves with branching veins and typically possess taproots. They are adaptable and resilient, thriving in various environments.
